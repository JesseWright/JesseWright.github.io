CS373 Spring 2017: Jesse Wright

Starting Out
January 23, 2017By: Jesse Wright

With the first week of classes over, I’m starting to learn the style of each of my professors for the classes I’m taking. At the same time, these first couple of weeks of class will set the tone for my final semester here at the University of Texas at Austin for the foreseeable future.

One of the courses I’m taking this semester is Software Engineering (CS373) with Glenn Downing. So far, the class has a much more industry feel to it, which I’m hoping will help prepare me for the not-too-distant working life. Before I start thinking too much about May, I have a few reflections about the first few days of my final semester here, primarily regarding Downing’s class.

What did you do this past week?

I started the week off simply, mostly spending my time preparing for classes by getting supplies and reviewing the upcoming schedules for my classes. I started my reading for CS373, but I later found out that the class schedule online doesn’t show the date something’s assigned but the date it’s expected due. Consequently, I struggled with Downing’s quiz this morning, having not finished the readings. Here I was thinking I was getting ahead.

What’s in your way?

Even a few classes in, I still feel rocky about my organization. Every semester I reset my habits and thinking to better suit the new semester. Maybe it’s the different style of Downing’s software-engineering class or all the tools he expects us to eventually know, but I seem a lot slower in adjusting to my classes. I guess I’m still trying to get into the groove of things.

What will you do next week?

Coming up, I’ll have twice the readings to finish, on top of the readings for my other classes, so I’ll spend most of my time reading textbooks. Downing also posted the information for our first project. I’d like to get ahead and finish that as early as possible.

My dad’s birthday is this weekend though, and I’d love to go up to DFW to visit him if I can since he’s turning 70. If I do go, I’ll almost certainly have to double down throughout the week to make sure I don’t get further behind.

What’s my experience of the class?

I’m not quite sure what to make of things yet. I enjoy the questions and the engaging teaching style Downing uses. The thought of daily quizzes makes me a little uneasy at times, but they’re also short and simple, much like the readings. The coming week or so will likely solidify my thoughts for the beginning of the class, especially with the upcoming project.

The content of the course, however, differs greatly from any other course I’ve taken — less college-style theoretical lessons, more practical tools and methods for actual software development. On one hand, having worked at my current internship for nine months now, I feel more than ready for the more practical style of Downing’s class. I’m comfortable with git and source control, agile development and the general design process for a product, and so on. On the other hand, I know this class will be both challenging and a lot of work. I don’t know what to expect going forward, really.

What’s my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

One thing I stumbled upon this past semester was an online service called Safari Books Online. It’s like the Netflix of books (and video and audio, actually) related to technology and coding. I originally decided to try it out after seeing it offered books from two of my favorite publishers, O’Reily and Manning. I figured a subscription would be a worthwhile investment to help me prepare for anything from Cisco certification exams to learning just about any computer language under the sun. As it would turn out, it also had every textbook I needed for my computer-science courses this semester. It’s something I’d recommend to any computer scientist or technologist.
